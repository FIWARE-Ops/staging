2025 Hackathon on Interoperability of Digital Public Infrastructure

The Hackathon on "Interoperability of Digital Public Infrastructure" is an exciting event where creative minds collaborate to solve one of today’s most pressing digital challenges. This event is co-organised by FIWARE Foundation in partnership with INPACE project, GLEIF, Gaia-X, in the context of the 3rd Symposium on Data for Public Good.

Digital Public Infrastructures (DPIs) are open, secure, and inclusive platforms — like digital identity, payments, and data exchanges — that power essential government and commercial services and can be scaled for global use. Designed to be interoperable, trusted, and built for the public good, DPIs enable seamless access and innovation.

The EU–India Trade and Technology Council (TTC) has made DPIs a top priority, recognizing their potential to build scalable digital foundations in developing countries and improve interoperability between European and Indian systems. India brings proven models in digital identity, e-payments, and secure data sharing, while the EU contributes its expertise in digital governance, infrastructure, and human-centric digital economies.

Challenge Objective

Develop a working prototype that enables seamless identity verification — for example, where an EU citizen uses a Digital Identity Wallet to access a data resource from an Indian organization registered in MOSIP (or vice versa).

Bonus Points for Integrating:

  • NGSI-LD for semantic data representation.
  • Data discovery and access via a connector such as Gaia-X.
  • Verifiable credentials through a vLEI server.
  • Digital identity frameworks relevant to Japan, South Korea, and Singapore.

Success Criteria

Your solution must show cross-border digital identity interoperability that:

  • Preserves data sovereignty;
  • Allows selective disclosure;
  • Works across heterogeneous DPI systems;
  • Complies with both GDPR and Indian data protection laws.

This Hackathon is open to all residents of India, Singapore, South Korea, Japan, European Union countries and Switzerland.
