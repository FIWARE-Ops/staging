EU-Indo-Pacific Digital Partnership Conference 2025

The EU–Indo-Pacific Digital Partnership Conference 2025, organized by INPACE, will bring together policymakers, researchers, innovators, and business leaders to deepen cooperation between the European Union and Indo-Pacific countries in the field of digital technologies and their practical applications.

The conference supports the implementation of the EU’s Digital Partnerships with Japan, South Korea, and Singapore, as well as the EU–India Trade and Technology Council, reinforcing shared goals in digital transformation and innovation.

Discussions will focus on key digital technologies, exploring new opportunities for collaboration in technology development, deployment, and governance — including policy alignment, standards, and regulatory frameworks that foster a secure, sustainable, and inclusive digital future.

Alberto Abella, COO of FIWARE Foundation, will contribute to two sessions:

Workship: “Innovation Convergence Lab: Where Technology Push Meets Market Pull” Topic: iHubs: a Global Network Fostering Co-creation (12:00-13:00 SGT) When: October 28, from 10:00 to 14:30 (SGT). This intensive co-creation workshop is designed to bridge the gap between cutting-edge technology and real-world market needs. Interdisciplinary teams of technology developers, policy experts, and industry leaders from Europe and the Indo-Pacific will collaborate to address two key challenges: AI for Sustainable Cities and Resilient Digital Infrastructure.

Session: “Human-Centric and Sustainable Infrastructures for Resilience of Public Services”** When: October 29, from 09:00 to 10:45 (SGT) Organized by Franck Le Gall (EGM), this session will explore how digital technologies and data-driven innovation can support cities and nations in achieving their net-zero carbon goals while enhancing quality of life. Discussions will feature real-world examples from Europe and the Indo-Pacific, highlighting progress in green digital infrastructures, federated local digital twins, identity management, and urban service innovation.
