German Indian Innovation Summit 2025

The German Indian Innovation Summit 2025 is a leading event organised by German Innovation Corridor (GIIC) to forge an Indo-European innovation corridor that will contribute to global prosperity and geopolitical stability. The mission is to consolidate the numerous roads and partnerships between Germany and India into a single express highway.

The German Indian Innovation Summit will bring together 300 stakeholders from politics, business, academia, and development cooperation to accelerate innovation partnerships, facilitate cross-border collaboration, and accelerate the implementation of digitalization efforts.

Achieving great success on the Indian technological market and becoming recognised as a National standard in India, FIWARE Foundation is proud to share its common vision and technologies based on Open Source and Open Standards, and be a part of the German Indian Innovation Summit 2025.

Chandra Challagonda, CEO at FIWARE Foundation, will attend a panel discussion titled “Beyond Boundaries: Unlocking the Future of Open Innovation India-Germany” to deep dive into:

  1. Understanding the use of common Indian and International Standards for interoperability, including usage within IoT systems and Data Spaces.
  2. How innovative Cross Domain technologies are applied in Smart Cities, energy mobility, manufacturing and other verticals to achieve seamless integration of data transfer from multiple sovereign participants.
  3. Standardisation is a key factor in being able to extract real value from context data and datasets, be it using AI or classical machine learning,
  4. Using FIWARE as a foundation/core to form the basis for the creation of scalable systems based on structured data, distributed trust, and security while maintaining data sovereignty.

Dynamic discussions with the leaders will also discover the most current topics in the global agenda, such as:

  • GreenTech & Sustainability: Green hydrogen, the circular economy, and climate data converge, driving resilient and regenerative prosperity.
  • Fin Tech: Open APIs harmonise Euro-Rupee transactions, fuelling inclusive fintech growth and regulatory innovation.
  • Cyber Phygital Security: Indo-German innovators co-develop quantum security, and interoperable autonomous systems.
  • Space Tech: Joint missions target lunar science, commercial small-sats, and additive engines propelling sustainability.
  • Startups & Digital Transformation: Transformation. Bilateral start-ups catalyse rapid digital transformation, bridging Mittelstand legacies with Indian scalability.
  • Mobility & Transport: Collaborative mobility ecosystem electrifies journeys, integrates hydrogen, and streamlines logistics across continents.
  • Technology / AI: German precision meets Indian data prowess to forge transformative, trustworthy AI leadership.
  • Manufacturing & Industry 4.0: Smart factories unite German engineering and Indian ingenuity, accelerating Industry 4.0 productivity revolution.
